Meet the New Chief of Google Chrome OS

Apr 1st, 2011

Well, it’s come.   Time for me to move on from Oaggle, Ubuntu and the rest of the Open Source World. As of 12:00AM April 1st 2011, I was hired by  Google to be their new head of the Chrome OS.

Before I get into details as to what I’ll be doing, I’d like to offer my congratulations to Roland Taylor as he becomes the head of 2Buntu, a new cloud-based competitor to Chrome OS. As part of this sudden change, I’m going to be implementing a different way of doing things in the Chrome OS Department. First, enough of this “Gentoo-based” stuff. The first thing we’re going to do is drop Gentoo (And by extension, the new Canterbury Distribution), and go with Google as our core. We’ll be running the same Debian-Gentoo-Fedora-Ubuntu-ARCH Beast that we run on our top-secret servers on Chrome OS.   Second, there’s the matter of HTML5. I’ve decided that Chrome OS won’t be supporting HTML5, and shall instead move straight to HTML6, which doesn’t need any coding at all. For more details on this new technology, please see this.   Third (And last), I’d like to thank the wonderful folks over at Oaggle.com for everything that they’ve done. You’re all awesome. Thanks Ryan and Conor.

Please note that today is April 1st. Thank you
